# Case-Study-2

This project focuses on modeling and simulating the vertical motion of a maglev train suspension system using MATLAB. A maglev train is levitated above the track using electromagnetic forces, where gravity pulls the train downward while magnetic attraction pulls it upward. The system maintains a small air gap between the train and the track, and the primary objective is to keep this gap stable during operation. However, the system is inherently unstable because any small increase in the gap reduces magnetic force and causes the train to fall, while a decrease in the gap increases magnetic force and pulls the train upward. Due to this instability, feedback control is required to maintain equilibrium.

To analyze the system, the original nonlinear model of magnetic levitation is simplified by first determining the equilibrium condition and then applying a small disturbance assumption. The system is linearized around this equilibrium point, allowing it to be represented as a standard second-order mechanical system. With the addition of control effects, the behavior of the maglev suspension becomes similar to that of a mass-spring-damper system, which is much easier to analyze and simulate.

The MATLAB implementation uses numerical methods to solve the system and observe how the train responds to small disturbances in the levitation gap. The simulation provides outputs such as displacement and velocity over time, helping visualize the dynamic response of the system. By adjusting system parameters, different damping conditions can be studied, including underdamped, critically damped, and overdamped responses, each showing distinct behavior in terms of oscillations and stability.
